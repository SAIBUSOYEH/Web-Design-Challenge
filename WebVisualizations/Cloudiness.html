Latitude vs. Cloudiness



Cloudiness is suppressed at the equator mainly due to equatorial ocean upwelling, hence atmospheric subsidence.

Continents tend to be less cloudy than adjacent oceans (at the same latitude), except in the equatorial belt. On average, cloudiness is highest around 60 S (about 80% of the time) but there is only 40% cloud cover at the South Pole. At latitudes above 50 N, the annual average cloud cover is about 70%.
